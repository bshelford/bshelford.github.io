Creating A Social Experience

Client: Crowd.Science

June 2017

Crowd.Science, a science crowdfunding startup based in Oxford Street, found massive success in funding campaigns for science research. But as each project ended they found the hard-won backers would vanish from their site leaving the following projects to the uphill struggle of gaining a following. They reached out to us to help them create ongoing social environment where scientists could share their knowledge with the public and create a community around them.

The Challenge

Crowd.Science found massive success in funding campaigns for science research. But as each project ended they found the hard-won backers would vanish from their site leaving the following projects to the uphill struggle of gaining a following. We needed to help them build an ongoing social environment by providing an engaging UX and clear purpose for this new area of the site.

Scientists often struggled to find time to interact on the site. They were often less social media savvy and intimidated by the idea of running their accounts.

Whereas people who had previously supported crowdfunding campaigns found that video was key to their engagement. That the content across the site needed to support the values they had.When asked if they would ask questions to the scientist they told us: “Would not dare post questions on that kind of thing. I know nothing about science and I would look like a joke”

To begin crowd.science road to a dynamic and exciting online science community, we designed, prototyped, tested, iterated and tested again. we focused our efforts on a clean, simple profile onboarding flow that helped users to create content as they went. We combined this with a category page that would allow users to find content that interested and engaged them, driving traffic to the scientists and the projects they were crowdfunding for.

At the end of the project, we had some areas that we felt would benefit Crowd.Science to focus on in the future:

  • Build in more social features to allow the community to interact in more ways.
  • Develop a mobile friendly version of the new features that fits with the rest of the site.
  • A lot of our early interviews indicated that users had a lot of trouble setting up and update their crowdfunding page, this therefore is an area of the site that would benefit from more development.
  • When users create a larger amount of content for the site, it would likely become necessary to re address the information architecture of the site.

Scientist Profile Page

Key discoveries made were:

Most users felt “Gamifying” the social side would unfairly reward those who had more time to spend on the site.
We also found that the Link campaign button need to be more obvious.
The create post interaction needed to be made smoother and easier.
