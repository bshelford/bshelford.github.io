Creating A New Banking Experience 

Client: Citi Bank

July 2017

Fin-tech is booming right now and the challenge to bring banking into the modern age is facing major banks around the world. In order to help meet this challenge Citi presented Intern team in 5 Critical Mass offices around the world with a design challenge for a mobile first banking product.But with the small team of three people and only three weeks it seemed as though our main challenge would be finding the right part of the problem to solve.

Insight

To begin to tackle this challenge we conducted desk research and then created an interview script based around the jobs to be done methodologies. Using this script we conducted face to face interviews with 12 people between the ages 18-35 to better understand their banking journey and the problems they experience.And we discovered that they are dissatisfied with there banks. They have new needs and frustrations that their traditional banking experience are not addressing. But like most people they couldn't be bothered to switch bank. So they answered these new frustrations by switching some of there banking activity to new players. They use Revolute for travel, Monzo for everyday life, Paypal for online spending, Splitwise for tracking spending across their flat. They mix and match these services too try and meet their needs. 

But its an imperfect system. They have security concerns with these start up banks, worrying if there money is safe and if they can get to it when they need it. On top of this coordinating between these apps is complicated and its hard to keep track of where all of their money really is. And to top it all off these apps are still buggy, experiencing service drop outs or systems they aren’t ready for more unique situations.

With these insights we gathered together and ran a design studio to ideate on how we might tackle the myriad of problems facing people. To focus the session we asked those joining to consider:

  • How might we create a personalised mobile banking exercise?
  • How might we create a social mobile banking experience?
  • How might we create an international bank account?
  • What is one thing you wish your bank could do for you?


The studio generated a ton of ideas which we took forward. Though the following week we began to focus the app to three concepts:

  • It will enable millennials to create and manage their own financial ecosystem

  • Millennials will be able to create shared accounts with friends to save for shared goals

  • It will connect the different service millennials want to use through open API

With these goals in mind we began I began wireframing the app and screen flow while our team visual designer created the branding and style for the app and our strategist created a marketing plan.

The goal for the visual design was to remove the serious face of the bank and instead give the app more of a friendly appeal. The citi bank colour where refreshed with our target market in mind, bringing in a lighter more energetic feel. The typeface was set in all lower case to give the app a feel of friendliness and relaxed conversation.

The app name was also created: Sembl. This was distilled from the word assemble as we wanted to have a name that presented the group/sharing nature of the app. But we also chose to show this as a brand powered by Citi and thus associated with the stability and safety attached to traditional banking.

We joined our two efforts into a high fidelity prototype built in Axure.

The Feed

The feed was designed to offer a view of the user’s finance, spending and sharing activity that is both easy to understand and familiar to navigate. Great for a quick update and for more detailed searches.

The action button is the users path through the app. It's floating position breaks from the screens layout making it stand out as a place to go to interact. Its designed to be easy, quick reach and to be in used in one gesture.

Shared Accounts

From here users will be able to create new accounts as easily as creating a whats app group.

Within the account each has access to a focused view of the feed showing them all the activity and progress in the account. Within the accounts screen they can see all the people involved and even invite new ones who want to tag along for the ride.

Accounts & Payments

But to best utilise a shared account you also need to understand your own financial health. SEMBL does this though the accounts screen.

Here users will be able to see all their account and total wealth all though the power of Open API. We also redesign payments to make sending over money as easy and painless as contactless cards have become.

The Result

After submitting our pitch my team where selected to pitch to the client executive team directly in New York alongside another Critical Mass team from Calgary. We received some excellent feedback from them, particularly around the insights we had collected and how we had executed our design based on them.
